# Category: executive-overview

**When it fires.** "Leadership dashboard", "are we on track", "delivery health at a glance", "weekly review", "risk summary". Tier 1.

**Decision it serves.** Product lead / exec: are we meeting commitments, what's the risk, where do I spend attention? Steering, not operating.

**Key panels + chart choices.**
- **Delivery success** — stat + sparkline + delta vs commitment (`16`). NOT a raw count.
- **Underdelivery risk** — forecasted $/% at risk, leading the status (`16`, `12` planner-impact).
- **Pacing stability** — oscillation index vs ceiling, sparkline.
- **Forecast quality** — calibration/accuracy vs baseline; the trust gauge for everything else.
- **Inventory utilization** — utilized/available against a target band.
- **Revenue impact** — $ delivered + $ at risk.
- All as a compact KPI grid; status by color **+ shape/label** (`94`).

**Drill paths.** Each KPI deep-links into the Tier-2 health view at the same time range + filter (delivery → delivery-explorer; forecast quality → forecast-explorer). One drill, then the exec surface stops.

**Recurring anti-patterns.** EXEC1 operational detail leaking in; EXEC2 KPI with no target; EXEC3 status without risk; SD1 vanity metrics. This category gets "noisy" exactly when these fire.

**Anchor metrics.** Delivery success %, underdelivery risk, forecast calibration, utilization, revenue at risk.
